AI Prompts for BFSI Sales Teams in India — Loans, MF & Wealth

Banking, Financial Services and Insurance (BFSI) is one of India's largest sales sectors with millions of salespeople across banks, NBFCs, insurance companies, and wealth management firms. AI prompts give BFSI sales teams a major edge — faster research, sharper objection handling, and better client communication. Here's how.

Personal loan sales — lead research prompt

Prompt: "I sell personal loans for a private bank in India. Write a B2C lead research guide targeting salaried employees in IT, manufacturing, and healthcare sectors in [city]. Include where to find them, what triggers a loan need, and 3 WhatsApp opening messages."

Mutual fund SIP — explaining to a first-time investor

Prompt: "A prospect has never invested in a mutual fund before. They are 30 years old, earning ₹60,000/month, and worried about market risk. Write a simple explanation of SIP in mutual funds in under 200 words. Use an analogy. End with a low-risk SIP recommendation pitch."

💡 Pro tip: Use the Pitch Generator tool to create a 30-second and 2-minute pitch for any BFSI product — just fill in your product name and target customer.

Credit card upsell — existing bank customer

Prompt: "I am calling an existing savings account customer to upgrade them to a premium credit card. Write a 90-second phone script. Start with their existing relationship, highlight 2 relevant benefits based on [spending pattern], and handle the 'I don't need a credit card' objection."

SME lending — cold email to business owner

Prompt: "Write a cold email to the owner of a small manufacturing business in Pune with 20–50 employees. I am offering a working capital loan up to ₹50 lakhs. Subject line, 100-word email, and a PS. Focus on speed of approval and minimal documentation."

Wealth management — high net worth client outreach

Prompt: "I am a relationship manager at a wealth management firm. Write an outreach message to a CXO at a mid-size IT company in Bangalore. I want to introduce our portfolio management services. Tone: peer-to-peer, not salesy. Under 80 words for WhatsApp."

Handling the 'I already have an advisor' objection

Use the Objection Handler tool, select 'Already working with someone else' and customise for BFSI. The 3-step response focuses on complementary value, not replacement — which works much better in the wealth management context.
