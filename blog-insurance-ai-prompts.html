ChatGPT Prompts for Insurance Agents in India — Complete Guide

Insurance is one of the toughest sales jobs in India — high rejection, long cycles, and complex products. AI prompts change the game by handling the research, objection scripts, and follow-up messages so you can focus on conversations. Here are the exact prompts that work.

1. Lead research prompt for life insurance

Paste this into ChatGPT: "I am an insurance agent selling term life insurance in India. My ideal customer is a salaried professional aged 28–45 in Tier 1 or Tier 2 cities, earning ₹8–25 lakh per year. Write me a B2C lead research guide — where to find these prospects, what pain points to address, and 3 personalised opening lines for WhatsApp outreach."

2. Objection handler — 'I already have insurance'

This is the most common objection you'll face. Use the Objection Handler tool and select 'Already have it' to get a 3-step response. The key is to acknowledge first, then ask a discovery question — never argue directly.

💡 Pro tip: Add the customer's current insurer name to your prompt for a more specific response. E.g. 'they have a ₹25 lakh LIC policy' changes the objection script completely.

3. WhatsApp follow-up sequence (5 messages)

Prompt: "Write a 5-message WhatsApp follow-up sequence for a prospect who showed interest in a term insurance plan but hasn't replied in 3 days. Tone: warm, helpful, not pushy. Each message under 60 words. Include 1 message that uses a recent news hook about rising medical costs in India."

4. Policy comparison explainer

Customers always compare. Use this prompt: "Create a simple side-by-side comparison between [Product A] and [Product B] for a first-time insurance buyer in India. Use simple language, no jargon. Highlight 3 reasons why [Product A] is better for someone with [specific customer profile]."

5. Renewal reminder message

Prompt: "Write a renewal reminder WhatsApp message for a customer whose policy expires in 30 days. Mention the risk of a lapse, the benefit of continuous coverage, and a clear call to action. Keep it under 80 words and friendly in tone."

6. Cross-sell script — existing customer

If your customer has life insurance, cross-sell health: "I have a customer who bought a term plan from me 2 years ago. They are 35, married with one child. Write a script to introduce them to a family health insurance plan. Start with their existing relationship, not with product features."

💡 Pro tip: Save your best prompts in the Sales Swipe File so you can reuse them across all your insurance prospects without retyping.
